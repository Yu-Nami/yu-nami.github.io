About

I am a PhD candidate in Polymer Materials at Zhejiang University, supervised by Prof. Jian Ji and Prof. Qiao Jin, and a visiting researcher in the Macromolecular Chemistry and Bavarian Polymer Institute (MCII) at the University of Bayreuth, working with Prof. Andreas Greiner and Prof. Seema Agarwal.

My research focuses on boronic-acid based supramolecular platforms to create kidney-targeted nano-antidotes, self-reporting drug carriers, and ROS-responsive materials for preventing nephrotoxicity and improving chemotherapy outcomes.

I am broadly interested in bridging polymer and supramolecular chemistry with drug product development, CMC/QbD, and translational nanomedicine.

Research

Supramolecular Platinum Catchers for Nephroprotection

Design of boronic-acid based supramolecular platinum catchers (SPC) that selectively bind cisplatin and its toxic metabolites, reducing renal accumulation while preserving anti-tumor activity.

Techniques: polymer synthesis, supramolecular assembly, in vivo nephrotoxicity models, ICP-MS

Self-Alarming Fluorescence-Enhanced Nanoantidotes (SAFEN)

Development of fluorescence-enhanced nanoantidotes that can report their activation and drug binding in real time, enabling intelligent management of drug-induced kidney injury.

Techniques: fluorescence spectroscopy, DLS/TEM, cell viability assays, animal imaging

Oxidative-Stress Responsive Boronate Networks

Construction of boronate–phenolic networks and ROS-responsive carriers for controlled release, kidney targeting, and combination therapy with cisplatin.

Techniques: boronate chemistry, gel networks, ROS-triggered release, pharmacokinetics

Polypeptide-Based Antimicrobial and Antitumor Systems

Design of polypeptide-based carriers (e.g., guanidinylated polylysine) for antimicrobial applications and combination therapies with chemotherapeutic agents.

Techniques: NCA polymerization, antimicrobial assays, structure–property relationships
